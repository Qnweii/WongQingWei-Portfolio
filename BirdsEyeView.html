OVERVIEW

Background

Jurong Bird Park was an aviary and tourist attraction located in Jurong.
It opened in 1971 and closed in 2022. It will be reopened in Mandai Wildlife Reserve and renamed to Bird Paradise.
This Virtual Reality app brings the user around some of the popular recreated areas in Jurong Bird Park, allowing them to experience/re-experience it. These areas are the Entrance arc, Ticketing Booth, Penguin Coast, Pools Amphitheatre, Parrot Paradise, Waterfall Aviary, and Lorry Loft. Each area will be designed and built similarly to the actual location.
There will also be different interactive activities at each area except the Entrance and ticketing booth for users to be immersed in.
Users get to earn points when successfully completing the activities. With these points, rewards like Singapore Zoo vouchers can be redeemed.

DESIGN RATIONALE

When discussing whether to develop a theme on educational content, the Jurong Bird Park concept came to our mind. We realized it holds one of our childhood memories as many of us had been to Jurong Bird Park when we were young, and this is an opportunity to create something memorable to commemorate the former Jurong Bird Park which has been closed.
Therefore, to remember Jurong Bird Park’s legacy during the 52 years, we decided to create a game about some of the most popular spots in Jurong Bird Park with missions to accomplish. This would allow players to go back to their times there and perhaps even evoke memories of the park's establishment, while participating in the game.

The ideal approach to recreate this Jurong Bird Park experience is to use a virtual reality (VR) headset since players can roam around and experience being in the Jurong Bird Park once more, which allows them to get more immersed in the experience.
One of the most important components in effectively replicating this VR game at Jurong Bird Park is the environment the player is in. Our main goal is to make the players feel nostalgic, and for us to do that, the environment they are in must be able to tell the story of the old Jurong Bird Park.
However, due to the time constraints, we were unable to accurately recreate the Jurong Bird Park in its entirety; as a result, the art concept of this VR environment we will be following is the stylized low poly.

Low poly style is preferred because it can be modelled and texture-mapped more quickly, and yet the environment's aesthetic appeal will remain fresh and innovative. We will be focusing more on the environment's structure by using a variety of shapes and forms but united through similar textures so that putting the elements together will still create the environment as closely as the Jurong Bird Park, as it will be difficult to replicate the feeling precisely due to the approach of using low poly style to recreate the Jurong Bird Park.

GAME OVERVIEW

There are five different activities the player has to complete at five areas.

PLATFORM MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS

A virtual reality (VR) headset and a Unity build of the game. It will be best if the player is using an Android-compatible desktop when downloading the Unity build of this game.

SYNOPSIS

Bird’s Eye View is a virtual reality experience which enables users to revisit the now closed Jurong Bird Park. The experience aims to bring an element of nostalgia to the user by recreating moments that they may have experienced at Jurong Bird Park.

The experience contains 5 different activities for users to interact with and experience, such as attending the Highflyer’s show at the Pools Amphitheatre and visiting the iconic Waterfall Aviary.

The experience allows the user to progress using a point system, each location would be linked with a task which they have to complete. Once the task is completed the user would earn points in which they are able to progress to the next location.

If the user's total points exceed 500, the user has the chance to receive a 50% discount on admission to any of the Singapore Wildlife Reserves' attractions, which are the Singapore Zoo, Night Safari, and River Safari.

In order to advertise this promotion, we created a poster and put it on display at the ticket counter so that user can learn about it and have a more immersive experience at the Jurong Bird Park even though it is a VR game we created.

We also developed a website which the user will be able to see their accumulated points as well as their profile information, gaming statistics, and screenshots from the VR game. Hence, the user can easily access their overall game information with the implementation of our website.

MAIN OBJECTIVE OF THE WEBSITE

The website was developed for registered players who can view their user information and game statistics from the VR game.
This website is linked to Firebase where all the data were fetched from there. Some statistics that they can look at will be how many points they will need to reach the reward and the quickest time taken to accomplish all the activity.

As each player has their own unique personal game statistics that they may refer to, this website will enable users to analyse their own game progress and enable them to better engage with the game.

Players can view their own screenshots taken using the camera asset in the game, as well as those taken by other players, on a different page. They can choose the pictures they prefer and send them to anyone they want through email or WhatsApp. Having a page where they can share screenshots from the game with one another will also help to better connect with other players.

The website allows users to simply update their new username and email address if they want to change their username and email address.
If their current points reach 500, they will receive a pop-up message congratulating them and more reward information will be emailed to them in the coming days so they can be informed about the reward they will be receiving soon.

Overall, this website benefits players in keeping track of their gaming progress, allows them to view pictures taken during their time playing, and also help to promote our game to whomever they share the pictures with.

GAME MECHANICS

1. Toggleable Map Asset
Players can teleport to different locations on this map, but they can only do so if the location is not locked. To finish the game, the player must complete each of the five areas on the map. When an activity is finished, they will need this map to move on to the next area.

2. Toggleable Camera Asset
With this camera, users can snap pictures of the places they like during the game, and afterward, the image will render out on the film above the camera so users can view the picture they just took.

3. Text-to-Speech Button
The text-to-speech functionality is located in the speaker icon in the upper right corner of the board stand. The vivid contrast blue colour is intended to help player notice the button clearly and understand what it does.


LEVEL SETTINGS

We have chosen to divide the playing areas into 5 separate levels. These levels are areas that were present in Jurong Bird Park. The design of these levels attempts to recreate a simplified version of the actual area in Jurong Bird Park. At each level, there is a different activity that must be finished before moving on to the next level.

This is done so that we can keep players engaged in various activities so that they will not become easily bored, and a different playing area will be made available to them after each activity is finished, giving them a new location to explore while completing activities. Each area will have a unique type of bird, with the exception of the ticket booth area, which serves as the starting location.

CONSIDERATIONS TO INCLUSIVE DESIGN

The main group of individuals that we are concentrating more on our VR game will be the ones with language barriers, who are also closely related to people of different ethnicities and those who are not very technologically savvy.

1. Language Barriers among people with different ethnicities
We chose to incorporate more icons into our game since they are a common visual language which effectively bridges language gaps. It will be preferable, compared to translation plug-ins, because it helps the player to identify quickly and easily what they need and want during the game.
Thus, utilizing icons will help a diverse range of individuals and improve visual interest and grab the player’s attention more easily.

GROUPS OF PEOPLE EXCLUDED BY OUR GAME

1. Groups that have a visual disability
The main purpose of our game was to do a small-scale recreation of Jurong Bird Park. Hence, the environment is a big part of our game. If the user has a visual disability, they aren’t able to fully enjoy/appreciate the experience.
Furthermore, the completion of the activities heavily relies on the visuals of the game. For example, the activity in Parrot Paradise, the user needs to be able to identify the position of the parrots to complete the activity. Hence, someone with a visual disability may not be able to complete the activities and move on to other locations.

2. Groups without modern technology
Our game is built for virtual reality. Therefore, a virtual reality headset is needed to play our game. If a user does not have a virtual reality headset, our game can’t be played.
