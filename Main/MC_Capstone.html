OVERVIEW

Background

Our Client, EN Group: Monster Curry, has identified certain areas for improvement in the Monster-EN business strategy.



After a comprehensive review of customer’s feedback, our team has decided to tackle the focus areas of Variety vs Efficiency and Portion Sizes, seeking to provide an enhanced and unique dining experience for our client’s patrons.

- Client's Concern: Variety vs Efficiency
Balancing innovation and customer desires against operational feasibility.



- Client's Concern: Portion Sizes
Striking the right balance between dining experience, product value, and patrons' beliefs with portion size.



3 Key Features

1. Enhancing the Customer Dining Experience with Augmented Reality (AR) Website

Focus Area: Portion Sizes

Our client has requested the incorporation of an AR experience in the customer dining journey to create an engaging and appetizing experience.
We have decided to allow customers to preview their food portions in AR before committing to a purchase. This also makes the portion size look less intimidating as they can get a good gauge of the portion.


2. Provide Entertainment through games while waiting for food. (2D Online Mobile Game: Remake the Curry!)

Focus Area: Variety vs Efficiency

In consideration of our client’s manpower concern, we have proposed the integration of exciting games aiming to entertain and immerse the customers during the lengthy wait time.
Overcoming boredom and restlessness, especially during peak hours. This is a mobile game where players can easily play through a website.



3. Improving the Food Selection Process with a Food Picker (Randomiser Website)

Focus Area: Variety vs Efficiency

To reduce the burden of selecting from a large catalog of food choices, we proposed implementing a random food picker feature.
This feature will help customers select their meals quickly and may also provide an exciting and playful way to choose meals. By simplifying choices, it avoids the phenomenon of choice overload and has the potential to increase customer satisfaction.



Target Audience

People in their late teens to early adulthood.

DETAILS OF THE 3 KEY FEATURES

1. AR Website

Due to the client's concerns of customers being intimidated by the portion sizes, we came up with a solution which is to implement an AR website for customers to be able to view the dish in AR before committing to the dish and purchasing it.
By allowing customers to preview the dish in AR, they are able to gauge the size of the dish, as well as see how the dish would look like when served.


In addition, the implementation of the AR Preview of the dish help promote the variety of the dishes that Monster Curry has to offer. As AR has not been widely implemented in the F&B industry, customers might be more inclined to view all the dishes in AR.
This would expose the customers to new dishes available in the menu, thus promoting the variety of Monster Curry's menu.


Possible User Journey Map for AR Website

We implemented Image Tracking in WebAR using Unity, which means users will be able to open up the website from their mobile devices, scan the image tracker, and the AR models will then show on the screen.

2. Online Mobile 2D Game (Remake The Curry!)

We develop a 2D single player game to entertain customers while they are waiting for their food. The game was made with the intention of giving the kitchen staff more time to prepare the dishes in demand.
While the customers plays the game, it keeps them preoccupied, making the waiting time seem shorter and overall improves their dining experience.

Estimated Game Duration: 5-10 minutes

The theme of the game is kitchen preparation. EN Group: Monster Curry, specializes in serving Japanese demi-glace curry. Hence, to fit their brand, the game is based on recreating a Japanese curry dish.
The players play the role of the chef preparing the dish. This allows the player to feel involved and creates a sense of personalisation, making the game more engaging and interactive.

Possible User Journey Map for 2D Game

We used Unity WebGL to develop the game, which means players will be able to launch the game by scanning the QR code from their mobile devices. It supports both Android and IOS devices.


How The Game Works

The game consists of 5 different cooking processes.
The first step would be Collection, followed by Frying, Boiling, Chopping, and lastly Plating.



*Note: The ingredients will be generated at random at the start, and it will determine the cooking processes throughout the game. Players will experience a unique cooking process each time they replay the game.

Players will be given 3 lives to complete the game, excluding the last step (plating), within the stipulated time.
Players can take their time to complete the last step.

3. Food Randomizer Website

Monster Curry offers a wide range of dishes for customers to choose from. This large variety may intimidate customers making them feel less keen to want to visit the store.
Thus, in order to counter this problem, we chose to implement a food randomizer website which will randomize any of the chef recommended menu items from selected category, so that customers can make quick food choices.

The randomizer allows customers to choose from 6 different categories; desserts, drinks, curry rice, pasta, curry ramen and monster burgers.
In addition, the randomizer would be able to filter the food items into different food categories such as vegetarian, seafood, chicken, pork and beef. This allows customers a more specific choice of randomization while also providing them an element of freedom in their choice.

KEY PROCESSES INVOLVED

Project Timeline



1. Using Photogrammetry for AR Website

One of the features we choose to do is an AR website that allows customers to see the life-size of the food dishes before placing their orders. We have two options to accomplish that: either we model the dishes manually or we utilize a 3D scanning photogrammetry tool to produce 3D models of the dishes.
It would be extremely tedious and time-consuming to physically model the dishes by ourselves, and they might not turn out to be an exact replica of the real food dishes, which would be detrimental to the client.

Therefore, using a 3D scanning photogrammetry software to produce 3D models of the dishes using /images taken from various angles will be the most obvious choice.

Polycam

After evaluating various photogrammetry applications, we conclude that Polycam is the best option because it is reasonably priced and simple to connect to the website using our phones.


We can also export the models in an appropriate file format, such as .obj and .fbx, from which we can import the models into Maya or Blender to make further improvements.


Photoshoot Meeting

We arranged a meeting with the client at one of the Monster Curry outlets for the photoshoot of all the featured dishes.
We used video capture on Polycam to capture the 9 dishes on an autonomous Lazy Susan rotating tray so that we do not have to move around constantly to shoot the dishes from different angles. We also took multiple shots of the dishes that generated poor 3D models.



When we were first testing out, we took over 100 /images for 1 dish and it took an hour to produce the 3D model, which was extremely time-consuming and inefficient.
As a result, we decided to take around 75–80 shots for each dish. Each 3D model of the dishes took around 30 minutes to generate, and the end results were of high and in acceptable quality.
We took a total of 3 hours to capture all the dishes with around 75-80 /images per dish.


Editing Process - Understanding Retopology and Quad Draw Techniques -



We remove unwanted rough edges after importing the models into Maya before conducting retopology and quad draw.


The purpose of retopology is to make the mesh simpler because the imported models from Polycam are high poly, which means that the polygons count is high. We want the polygon counts to be as low as possible so that it helps to minimize lag time when the model is being viewed online.
Therefore, retopologizing will help lower the poly count and make the mesh look more structured.

After the retopology process, the mesh is more organized and the number of polygons is smaller. The following stage will involve using the quad draw technique to fill up any holes discovered on the low poly mesh.

The last step will be baking the high poly to low poly mesh, which results in an organized mesh with lesser polygons that is prepared for import into Unity.

User Persona



User Testing Improvements

Problem 1

Problem: Detection of the image tracker is challenging due to the large size of AR models

After user testing, we discovered that many users had trouble scanning due to the large size of the AR models. In order to see the AR models clearly as a whole plate, they had to move their phones further away from the image tracker.



If they moved the phone farther away from the image tracker, it can cause the image tracker to lose detection. Because of this, users can get frustrated because the AR models keep disappearing from their phones and need to be rescanned repeatedly.

However, the AR models are intentionally made to be the life-size of the plates as the purpose is to enable users to understand the actual size of the plates before ordering.

Improvement: Include a new feature that allows users to resize the AR models

To avoid the need for constant phone movement in order to view the entire AR dish, we decided to add a new function that will allow users to resize the dish using their fingertips.

In order to inform users of the difference between the ratio of the actual size compared to the one that they have scaled, we also put a notification text at the bottom.
The notification text shows the proportion of the actual size in relation to the scaled version. This also serves as a reminder to the user that the scaled version does not represent the dish's actual size.



Problem 2

Problem: The users were unaware that they could rotate/scale the dish using their fingertips

Users were unaware they could rotate/scale the dish to see different angles when we asked them to do so. Because of this, they got confused by the 'Reset dish' button, and they were curious about how it worked.



Therefore, we have to make the instructions more straightforward and ensure that they are aware of the dishes being able to rotate/scale.

Improvement: Include arrows (UI) on the screen to indicate that the dish can be rotated/scaled

We decided to add an arrow indicator at the corner of the screen to inform users that they can rotate/scale the dish with their fingertips to view it from various perspectives.
We positioned it in the corner to prevent it from taking up too much space on the screen, which would block the view of the AR model.



Problem 3

Problem: User did not know that there was a spice level VFX beside the AR model

From user testing, we realise that after the user chose their spice level, they were confused about what was supposed to happen. We had to tell the user there was a Fire VFX beside the model and they had to move back the phone to see it.



Improvement: Add text to prompt user to move further away to see spice level UI

With this text, user would know to move further away to see the Fire VFX result.

User Testing Improvements

Problem 1

Problem: The colors of the home page differ from those used in the Monster Curry company color scheme

After inviting clients to play the game, they requested that the home page's colors be changed because they do not match the Monster Curry brand's color scheme.
In order to convey to customers that the game is from Monster Curry, they want to use more of their trademark colors on the game's home page.



Improvement: Change the colors of the title of the game

We changed the colors on the home page using the color scheme that they provided us. We will only be changing the title bar colors as it highlights the name of the game.



Problem 2

Problem: There is no clear explanation of what this game is about, and it is unclear how will it be played

We discovered during the user testing that many of them do not understand how the game works and are unsure of how it will be played. They are unclear about the overall game's objectives and how many levels there are.
This is a major issue because the users do not know what they are playing, and they are just playing without having any knowledge of the context of the game.

Although there are instructions provided for how to play each level, the primary goal of the game is not made clear.



Improvement: Display a game progress map before the start of each level

After thorough client feedback and user testing, we made the decision to add a game progress map before the start of each level so that players will know how many levels remain before the game is completed.

This helps players to understand the different levels they will be completing and the general flow of the game, so as to avoid confusion and get them ready for the next level they will be playing.
