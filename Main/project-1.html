Project:
Recommending Meal Kit Based On User's Emotions

Project Task
To design and create both mobile & smartwatch app prototype for Post-Covid 19
Project Type
Individual project
Project Goals
To support food and beverage (F&B) businesses
To assist individuals under quarantine
To improve the mood of the individuals
Tool
Figma
Miro
Lucidchart
Duration
3 weeks

OVERVIEW

Background

Due to the negative impact had on F&B businesses during Covid-19, establishing a platform where users can continue to support local businesses from the comfort of their own homes will be ideal.
‍
Thus, this project I am working on will be based on meal kit app that recommends ready-to-cook meals based on user’s emotions on that day, which will recommend the right food choices for them to improve their mood and cater to their needs at the same time.
Name of app: Reverse Color Plate

Logo Design

This logo was created primarily to depict user’s emotions on that day and recommend the right meal kits for them to improve their mood.

PROCESS

Design Process

EMPATHIZE PHASE

Competitive Analysis

Competitive Analysis is a strategic management that assess strengths and weakness of current and potential competitors. I did a research looking for similar meal kit apps and compared 3 apps of which 1 of them is strong.
Overall, these three meal kit apps will be ranked as follows: BlueApron is first, HelloFresh is second, and last but not least, HomeChef is third. All three apps have advantages and disadvantages, but BlueApron strikes a good balance between user experience and interface.

Qualitative Analysis

I interviewed some of my friends and family members who are students and working adults. I interviewed them over video calls and phone calls by using zoom, WhatsApp, Microsoft teams platforms. In total, there were 8 participants with whom I had the opportunity to interview them and gather insights about their challenges and motivations.

DEFINE PHASE

User Persona

This persona represents the ideal customer for my app based on all the information gathered by the research and interviews.

Empathy Map

In order to get a deeper understanding about User Behaviors and decisions, I used an Empathy Map to articulate what I know about the user and elaborate further on my user persona.

User Journey Map

Based on the User research, I have created a User Journey map to better understand the crucial touchpoints, mindsets (emotions) and overall journey which user may encounter when ordering a meal kit while being allergic to certain food.

A 'Day in the life of' storyboard

To better understand the motivations for my persona's behaviors and action, I created a storyboard that will provide insights of what she think and do outside the scope of interaction with my app. It will depict her typical daily activities and how she can use the app on her daily basis.

IDEATION PHASE

User Flows

These user flows will be the path taken by the user when using this app to complete a certain task.

Scenario 1

User would like to purchase meal kit

Scenario 2

User wants to add quarantine meal kit to cart

Scenario 3

After dining in at a local food business outlet, the user wishes to enter the promo code from the receipt into the app

Scenario 4

User wants to view his past meal purchases

Card Sorting

Before building the structure for my app, I used the card sorting technique to help me organize my content after researching about my user. This helps me to plan out my structure easily and understand my user's expectations before prototyping the final product.

PROTOTYPING PHASE

Paper Prototype

Once I organized all my insights from the Ideation phase, I started to sketch several of the app's main screens , using my user flows as my guide and this allows me to quickly explore several concepts for the screen layouts. Afterwards, I used the low-fidelity wireframes that I drew, and guide the designs through paper prototyping to imagine how users would navigate the app to reach the end goal.

TESTING PHASE

User Feedbacks

I used the final prototype to conduct a test with 3 users within the target group. Users were able to use the app with ease and are satisfied with the designs. However, there were a few positive and negative feedbacks. This helped me to uncover future opportunities to improve the overall user experience by building an efficient, effective and satisfied experience for my users.
