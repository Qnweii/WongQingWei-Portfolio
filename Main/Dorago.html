PRINTING PROCESS

Because of the intricate nature of Dorago's model, I found it necessary to disassemble certain body parts for the printing process. This step was taken to ensure the success of the print, prevent any potential surface quality issues caused by support structures, and also to optimize the printing time.


The body of Dorago can still be printed as a whole as it has few supports and can be removed easily.

CHALLENGES FACED

The printing of Dorago's body alone required a total of 9 hours and 35 minutes.
With all the separated parts combined, it took a total of 22 hours to complete printing Dorago!!
Halfway through the printing, there was shortage of white filament, so I had to use a grey filament to continue printing the rest of Dorago’s main body.

PAINTING TECHNIQUES

After some online research regarding painting PLA 3D prints, it is recommended to apply primer first before acrylic paint.
Hence, I started by sticking the tail, nails, and horns onto Dorago’s body using hot glue before painting and priming, to ensure an even distribution of colours. Afterwards, I used an airbrush to apply primer in layers to the 3D prints.


For the remaining body parts, I used acrylic and painted it individually.
